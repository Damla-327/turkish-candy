THE STORY:

The famous Italian chef Cristoforo di Messisbugo mentioned rice pudding in his 1549 book, describing it as "Turkish-style rice soup." Back then, rice pudding was more liquid and consumed hot, with sugar sprinkled on top rather than mixed in. In the 16th century, Italian sources described it as a dish made with sugar, milk, rice, rose water, and cinnamon, showing variations in ingredients and consistency. Over time, rice pudding evolved into the dessert we love today. This dessert, originating from the Ottoman Empire, is now enjoyed in Western and Northern Europe, particularly in Scandinavia where it's served with almonds and cinnamon on New Year's Eve. In Turkey, rice pudding is cooked on the stovetop or in the oven, achieving its desired consistency with rice flour."
