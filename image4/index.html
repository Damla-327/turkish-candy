THE STORY:

Rock candy is one of the oldest types of sweets/candy in Turkish and Ottoman cuisine.  The history of sugar, which some people call Konya sugar or Mevlana sugar today, dates back to the time of the Ottoman Empire. The word akida comes from the Arabic word akit and means 'commitment, contract, attachment, inseparability'. Actually, considering the hard structure of rock candy, this name sounds quite meaningful. During the Ottoman period, rock candy was served to the “yeniceri”soldiers at the ulufe ceremony and to important people of the state. It was thought to symbolize the state with its hard and colorful structure. By eating the hard candies offered at the Ulufe ceremony, the soldiers proved their loyalty and obedience to the state and made a promise. This candy, which was very popular during the Ottoman period, has a place in our memories as the candy that was often served to children and guests who came to celebrate the holidays.
