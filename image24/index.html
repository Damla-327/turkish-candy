THE STORY:

According to one story, the Ottoman Sultan summoned his confectionary chef to create a sweet treat that was easy on his teeth and throat, and that he could use to impress women and restore peace in the palace. The Sultan and his wives loved the sticky sweet, and he ordered a regular supply. This story is considered the most romantic version of how Turkish delight originated

TEXTURE:

Sultan Turkish delight, also known as lokum, is a traditional confection made from a mixture of sugar and starch that has been a staple of Turkish cuisine since 1777. The result is small, fragrant jelly cubes that are often flavored with rosewater, orange flower water, or citrus fruit juice, and dusted with icing sugar. Traditional varieties may also be flavored with vanilla, pomegranate, orange, or lemon.
